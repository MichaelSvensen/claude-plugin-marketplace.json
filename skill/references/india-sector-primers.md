# India Sector Primers — Market Structure at a Glance

Fast, partner-level orientation for thirteen Indian sectors: the market shape, who leads, the value chain, the KPIs to speak in, the regulator, and the recurring case tension. Use immediately after problem framing on any India engagement or case, alongside `references/india-corporate-houses.md` (who the incumbents are) and `references/industry-heuristics.md` / `references/industry-intelligence.md` (global sector economics). Companion to `references/industry-heuristics.md`.

**All figures are approximate, directional benchmarks** for structuring, not sourced facts — market sizes and growth rates move, so refresh before quoting to a client and label anything you extrapolate `[ILLUSTRATIVE]`.

## Automobile
- **Snapshot.** ~$220B, world's 5th-largest market; four segments — two-wheelers and passenger vehicles dominate domestic demand, plus commercial vehicles and tractors. 2W outsell cars ~5:1.
- **Leaders.** Maruti Suzuki (car leader), Hyundai, Tata Motors, Mahindra (SUV/utility); 2W — Hero, Honda, TVS, Bajaj.
- **Value chain.** R&D → raw material → inbound logistics → manufacturing → outbound → dealer distribution → marketing → after-sales (service + parts, a high-margin annuity).
- **KPIs.** Volume growth, segment mix, capacity utilization, dealer inventory days, EBITDA margin, EV penetration.
- **Case tension.** The ICE→EV transition (capex + new competitors), rural demand cyclicality, and the after-sales profit pool. Watch localization/PLI incentives.

## Aviation
- **Snapshot.** 3rd-largest domestic market; low-cost-carrier led and now highly consolidated (IndiGo dominant; Air India–Vistara under Tata is #2). Segments: airlines, airports, MRO & ground handling.
- **Value chain.** Flight ops · ground ops · MRO (operational); revenue management · distribution · customer experience (commercial). Airport ownership splits AAI-managed vs. private-concession (Adani, GMR).
- **KPIs.** RASK/CASK, load factor, ASK growth, fuel cost % of opex (~40%), fleet utilization, on-time performance.
- **Case tension.** Thin margins squeezed between fuel + lease costs and price-sensitive yields; route/network economics; the airport-privatization wave. A mandate for ~1% sustainable aviation fuel is on the horizon.

## Cement
- **Snapshot.** ~$143B; India is the world's 2nd-largest producer (~7%+ of global capacity). Top-4 players hold ~75%. Demand splits roughly urban/rural/infrastructure.
- **Process flow.** Limestone (extracted) → raw meal → clinker (kiln) → cement (+ gypsum) → bagged → retailers. Energy and freight are the cost swing factors.
- **Leaders.** UltraTech (Aditya Birla) #1, then Adani (ACC + Ambuja, acquired 2022 — the largest Indian infra M&A), Shree, Dalmia.
- **KPIs.** Capacity utilization, realization (₹/tonne), freight radius, power & fuel cost/tonne, blended EBITDA/tonne.
- **Case tension.** A commoditized, freight-bound, consolidating market — the game is utilization, cost position, and regional pricing discipline.

## E-commerce
- **Snapshot.** ~$75B and compounding fast; models span marketplace, inventory-led, and quick-commerce. Categories led by electronics, fashion, and grocery.
- **Leaders.** Flipkart (Walmart) and Amazon at the top; Meesho on the value tier (zero-commission, social/reseller distribution); Blinkit/Instamart/Zepto in quick-commerce.
- **Value chain.** Sourcing/selection → catalog → demand generation → fulfilment/logistics (the cost battleground) → last mile → returns.
- **KPIs.** GMV, take rate, AOV, contribution margin per order, CAC/LTV, delivery cost per order, repeat rate.
- **Case tension.** Growth vs. contribution margin. India e-commerce cases live or die on **unit economics per order**, not GMV. FDI rules constrain inventory-led models for foreign players.

## Financial services (ex-banking)
- **Snapshot.** Deep and digitizing; the stock market is projected to approach ~$5T (top-5 globally). Segments: capital markets (broking, IB, AMC), insurance (life/non-life), and NBFCs.
- **Benchmarks.** MF AUM ~$478B with only ~5–6% penetration (long runway); insurance AUM heading toward ~$1T. FDI in insurance raised to 74%.
- **Leaders.** Bajaj Finance (NBFC), HDFC/ICICI Pru & LIC (insurance), and AMC majors.
- **Regulators.** RBI (NBFCs), SEBI (markets), IRDAI (insurance).
- **KPIs.** AUM growth, NIM, credit cost/GNPA (lending), combined ratio (insurance), expense ratio, cross-sell.
- **Case tension.** Under-penetration = growth, but the swing factor is **credit cost and distribution efficiency**; digital acquisition and account-aggregator rails are reshaping economics.

## Banking
- **Snapshot.** ~$2.7T in assets; public-sector banks hold ~60%. India runs a world-leading digital-payments stack — roughly 40% of global digital transactions, dominated by UPI.
- **Structure.** Scheduled commercial banks (public, private, foreign) + small-finance/payments banks + a large fintech overlay (~$150B).
- **KPIs.** NIM, CASA ratio, cost-to-income, GNPA/NNPA, credit growth, CET1/capital adequacy, RoA/RoE.
- **Regulator.** RBI (CBDC pilot, Retail Direct, account-aggregator framework in play).
- **Case tension.** Private banks taking share from PSBs; monetizing UPI's zero-MDR funnel via cross-sell to lending; asset quality through the cycle.

## FMCG
- **Snapshot.** ~$110B; famed for deep rural distribution. Segments: food & beverage, home care, personal care, healthcare. E-commerce/D2C is the fastest channel (~28% CAGR).
- **Leaders.** HUL, ITC, Nestlé India, Britannia, Dabur; a wave of D2C challengers in personal care and F&B.
- **KPIs.** Volume vs. value growth, gross margin, A&P spend %, distribution reach (outlets), rural/urban mix, direct-reach %.
- **Case tension.** Premiumization and D2C disruption vs. mass rural distribution moat; input-cost (palm oil, crude derivatives) pass-through; the general-trade→modern-trade→quick-commerce channel shift.

## Iron & steel
- **Snapshot.** India is the world's 2nd-largest crude-steel producer. Products split flat (auto, appliances) vs. long (construction). Coking coal (largely imported) is the input swing factor.
- **Leaders.** JSW Steel, Tata Steel, SAIL, ArcelorMittal Nippon Steel (AM/NS), Jindal Steel & Power; Vedanta in adjacent metals.
- **KPIs.** Crude-steel capacity/utilization, realization/tonne, EBITDA/tonne, coking-coal cost, capacity-expansion pipeline.
- **Case tension.** A global-price-cycle taker with heavy fixed costs; the levers are cost position (captive iron ore, energy), integration, and disciplined capacity build. Green-steel transition is the long-horizon theme.

## IT / ITES
- **Snapshot.** ~$200B+ in exports; India is the world's #1 offshoring/services hub. Segments: IT services, BPM/ITES, engineering R&D, product/GCCs.
- **Leaders.** TCS, Infosys, Wipro, HCLTech, Tech Mahindra; plus a booming global-capability-center (GCC) footprint.
- **KPIs.** Revenue growth (CC), EBIT margin, utilization, attrition, deal TCV/book-to-bill, revenue per employee, offshore mix.
- **Case tension.** Wage inflation and attrition vs. margin; dependence on discretionary client spend (US/Europe); and the **GenAI disruption** to the linear headcount-growth model — the pyramid-to-productivity shift.

## Metals & mining
- **Snapshot.** A top global producer of aluminium and zinc; also copper, iron ore, and coal. Highly concentrated and capital/energy-intensive.
- **Leaders.** Hindalco/Novelis (aluminium, Aditya Birla), Vedanta/Hindustan Zinc, NALCO, Coal India (coal monopoly), NMDC (iron ore).
- **KPIs.** Cost of production/tonne (cost-curve position), LME-linked realization, captive power/ore integration, capacity utilization.
- **Case tension.** LME-price takers where survival is set by **cost-curve position**; captive power and vertical integration are the differentiators; ESG/decarbonization pressure on smelting.

## Oil & gas
- **Snapshot.** India is the 3rd-largest energy consumer and imports ~80%+ of crude (imports ~$120B); the 4th-largest LNG importer. Chain: upstream (E&P) → midstream (storage/transport) → downstream (refining/retail).
- **Leaders.** ONGC (#1 upstream), Indian Oil / BPCL / HPCL (downstream PSUs), Reliance (integrated, Jamnagar), GAIL (gas).
- **KPIs.** Gross refining margin (GRM), refinery utilization, upstream reserves/production, marketing margin, import dependence.
- **Case tension.** Import-price and geopolitics exposure (crude sourcing diversified toward Russia/Guyana); refining-margin cyclicality; and the capital pivot into the energy transition (green hydrogen, biofuels).

## Power
- **Snapshot.** ~411GW installed capacity vs. ~205GW peak demand; India is the 3rd-largest producer/consumer. Mix ~57% thermal, ~30% renewables, plus hydro/nuclear. National target ~500GW non-fossil.
- **Leaders.** NTPC (largest generator), Adani Power, Tata Power, JSW Energy; renewables led by Adani Green, ReNew, Tata Power.
- **Chain.** Generation → transmission → distribution (discoms, the stressed link) → retail. Governed by the Electricity Act, 2003; regulated by CERC/SERCs; PPAs set economics.
- **KPIs.** PLF (plant load factor), tariff (₹/kWh), AT&C losses, discom receivable days, renewable capacity adds.
- **Case tension.** Renewables shift + discom financial distress (receivables, AT&C losses) are the twin themes; PPA structure and grid integration drive returns.

## Telecommunication
- **Snapshot.** ~$37B; world's 2nd-largest subscriber and internet base. A consolidated **3-player** market after brutal price wars. Segments: mobile, fixed-line/broadband, enterprise.
- **Leaders.** Jio (Reliance), Bharti Airtel, Vodafone Idea (financially stressed) + BSNL.
- **KPIs.** ARPU (the key repair metric), subscriber net adds, data usage/sub, spectrum + capex intensity, churn.
- **Case tension.** ARPU repair after tariff wars, heavy 5G/spectrum capex, and the 3-player market's stability (Vodafone Idea's viability). Monetizing 5G and the digital-services layer on top of connectivity is the growth question.
